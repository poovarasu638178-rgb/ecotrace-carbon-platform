Step 1: Transport & Mobility
Petrol cars emit ~0.21 kg CO₂/km. EVs emit ~0.05 kg CO₂/km — switching saves ~1.5 tons/year.
Driving 40km/day in a petrol car adds ~3 tons CO₂ annually — equal to burning 1,400 liters of fuel.
Buses emit ~0.089 kg CO₂/km per passenger — 2.4× less than a solo petrol car. Trains are even better at 0.041 kg/km.
A single short-haul return flight emits ~0.5 tons CO₂ — equivalent to driving for 2 months.
A long-haul return flight (e.g., Mumbai–London) emits ~1.5 tons CO₂ per passenger.
Step 2: Diet & Food Consumption

🍖 Meat Lover

Red & white meat daily. High emissions — ~3.3 tons CO₂/yr.

🥗 Average Meat

Balanced diet with poultry & beef — ~2.5 tons CO₂/yr.

🥛 Vegetarian

No meat, includes dairy & eggs — ~1.7 tons CO₂/yr.

🌱 Vegan

100% plant-based. Lowest emissions — ~1.5 tons CO₂/yr.

Switching from a meat-heavy diet to vegan diet saves up to 1.8 tons CO₂/year. Diet is one of the single biggest lifestyle changes you can make.
Food waste in landfills produces methane — 80× more potent than CO₂. Reducing waste by 50% can save ~0.2 tons/year.

Prefer Local & Seasonal Produce

Reduces transport emissions associated with imports

Locally sourced food travels 90% less distance — cutting food-transport emissions significantly. Seasonal produce also avoids energy-intensive greenhouse growing.
Step 3: Home Energy & Household
India's grid emits ~0.82 kg CO₂/kWh. Using 250 kWh/month equals ~2.46 tons/year. LED lighting can cut usage by 70%.

Using Renewable Energy

Solar, wind, or green tariff electricity

Switching to 100% renewable energy at home can eliminate 2–4 tons CO₂/year — one of the highest-impact individual actions.
More people sharing a home = lower per-person emissions from heating, cooling, and appliances. Shared households are more efficient.
Step 4: Consumer & Lifestyle
The fashion industry emits ~10% of global CO₂. Each clothing item averages ~11 kg CO₂ to produce. Buying secondhand or repairing items reduces this drastically.
Manufacturing a smartphone emits ~70 kg CO₂. Extending device life by 1 extra year can cut its carbon footprint by 30%.
Streaming HD video emits ~0.036 kg CO₂/hour. Data centres consume ~1% of global electricity. Lowering video quality on mobile saves energy.
